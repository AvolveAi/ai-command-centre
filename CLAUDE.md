## How I Like to Work

### Before Starting Any Task
- For anything with 3+ steps: tell me your plan before you start. I will approve it.
- If something is ambiguous: ask ONE focused question — don't guess and proceed
- If you must make an assumption: state it as ASSUMPTION: [X] so I can catch it
- Never resolve conflicting requirements silently — surface them immediately

### While Working
- Only touch what's needed for the task. No side-quests.
- If you notice something else that needs fixing: note it, don't fix it without asking
- If something goes sideways: stop and re-plan. Don't keep pushing.

### When You're Done
- Give me a 1-line summary of what changed, then stop
- Don't explain what you just did unless I ask
- Don't narrate what you're about to do — just do it
- Exception: for irreversible actions or big decisions, explain before executing

### When You Make a Mistake
- Acknowledge it, adjust, apply the lesson going forward
- "I assumed X" after the fact is not acceptable — ask before acting
